How do solar panels work?

Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels are composed of solar cells that convert sunlight into direct current (DC) electricity. The DC electrical charge is then converted into alternating current (AC) electricity by a power inverter so that it can be used for your electricity needs.

What benefits do Solar provide?

1. Zero electricity bills. 2. No environment pollution. 3. Low maintenance cost. 4. Best kind of Investment. 5. Increases the value of Properties.

How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels have an expected lifespan of at least 25 years (or longer), solar panels suffer a 0.5% to 1% efficiency loss every year. At the end of a 25-year warranty, your panels should still produce energy at 75-87.5% of their rated output.

Where can such plants be installed?

Such rooftop systems can be installed at the roofs of residential and commercial complex, housing societies, community centres, government organizations, private institutions etc.

How will I know if solar energy can work for me?

Solar panels work best when they are installed facing south or west. If you have a large area of roof that faces either south or west and does not receive significant amount of shading, your place is probably suitable for rooftop solar panels.

What size solar system will work best for me?

The size of your system will depend on your current energy usage, the level of energy efficiency of your home or business, the available usable roof space, your budget and financing options, and the percentage of your energy consumption you want to offset with solar.

What is Net Metering?

The grid connected rooftop system can work on net metering basis wherein the beneficiary pays to the utility on net meter reading basis only. Alternatively two meters can also be installed to major the export and import of power separately. The mechanism based on gross metering at mutually agreed tariff can also be adopted.

How much roof area is required to set up the grid connected rooftop solar system?

About 10sq.m area is required to set up 1 kWp grid connected rooftop solar system.

How much energy will my solar system generate?

A well-situated 1 kW solar PV array will produce approximately 4 units per day and 1460 units of electricity per year.

What is Feed-in-Tariff?

In feed-in-tariff the Government offers a tariff for purchase of the solar power generated from such plants.

Do solar panels work in the winter?

Solar panels operate perfectly well in the winter, especially if you have good sun exposure and many sunny days a year. Winter means fewer hours of daylight, but most homes use much less electricity in the winter, that is a factor also.

Among net metering and feed-in-tariff what is preferred?

Net metering mechanism is more popular among States.

What kind of maintenance is required for solar panels?

There are no moving parts in a standard rooftop solar panel, so there is not much that will break or malfunction under normal conditions. The thin film of dust blocks a little bit of light from hitting the panels, reducing the amount of electricity they make, so solar panels should be sprayed down periodically with warm water to prevent dust and ensuring that your solar panels are operating at their maximum potential. Most panels have a 25 years lifetime, and solar inverters lifetimes range from 5 to 15 years.

What is a Grid Connected (On Grid) Solar System?

A grid-tied system is a solar PV system connected to the utility grid, which allows customers to receive electricity from both the solar PV system and the utility.

What is a Solar Rooftop System with Storage facility (Off Grid)?

Such rooftop system has battery as storage facility. The solar electricity is stored in the battery and can be utilized during night also when the sun is not available.

How much does solar cost?

The cost of solar depends on the Quality of materials, application, technology, size and area, electricity consumption and type of plant you are adopting. Cost varies from residential to industrial customers. An Important factor to decide the cost is Government incentives.

What is the return on investment like?

The time it takes you to recover your investment will depend on the technology you select, your energy efficiency and usage habits, your current and future electricity rates, the system size and configuration, and available incentives.Depending on your usage and the size system you put in, you could see a return of 3-7% on your investment in the first year – a very good rate. Solar is one of the few things consumers can purchase that does provide a financial return on your investment; imagine what the ROI would be for your car that you fill with gas every week. Investing in solar today helps you mitigate the future risks, fuel volatility, and uncertainty, while also improving your self-reliance and energy independence.

In case of grid failure, is there any chance for shocks to the person who is repairing?

In case the grid fails, the solar power has to be fully utilized or stopped immediately feeding to the grid so as to safe-guard any grid person/technician from getting shock (electrocuted) while working on the grid for maintenance etc.
